\chapter*{Abstract}
\thispagestyle{empty}

Patients often continue performing exercises outside of the clinic (for instance at home, thus without any therapist's supervision), after completion of the in-clinic physical therapy.
Aiming to sustain the accurate execution of the rehabilitation tasks, patients should receive similar feedback, as otherwise provided by a physical therapist, for the execution of these unsupervised exercises. 
To address this problem, several approaches have been proposed using feedback mechanisms for aiding rehabilitation. Unfortunately, test subjects frequently report difficulty in completely understanding the provided feedback, therefore failing to correctly execute the given movement.
This paper presents SleeveAR, a novel approach to address new real-time, active feedback strategies, and the application of multiple projection surfaces for providing user feedback.
Empirical evaluation compared to traditional video-based feedback evidence improvements brought by the proposed system , suitable to successfully guide a subject through an exercise prescribed (and demonstrated) by a physical therapist.
